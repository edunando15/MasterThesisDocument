\chapter{Problem Statement}
\label{chap:problemStatement}

Internet of Things (IoT) is a term denoting interconnection of devices connected to a network,
capable of communicating via different means \cite{berte_defining_2018}.

As the presence of IoT devices becomes more ubiquitous and pervasive, the need to handle the generated by these devices acquires more and more importance.
The data generated by IoT devices is often referred to as \textit{IoT data} and is characterised by its high volume, velocity, and variety.
The scope of this research encompasses Architecture, Engineering, and Construction (AEC) industry, where Building Information
Modelling (BIM) has become a standard practice for developing smart buildings, that are facilities equipped witha variety of IoT devices to monitor and control various aspects of the building's operation.

The need to handle IoT data results in the massive use of IoT platforms, that are a combination of hardware and software infrastructures that allow user to manage, process, and visualise IoT data \cite{babun_survey_2021} \cite{barros_anatomy_2022}.

Literature review revealed that the deployment of IoT solutions into IoT platforms involves a significant amount of manual work, that is often time-consuming and error-prone.
That is why research focussed on the automation of such procedures, using ontology-based approaches (...) or tool-based approaches (...).
